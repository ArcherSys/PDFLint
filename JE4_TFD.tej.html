"Indeed , she takes out more papers than I have ever seen her do in other places: my birth certificate, my baptismal record, a doctors letter concering my bout with chicken pox,rent reciepts, records of immunization, a letter about our public assistance payments, even her marraige license - every single paper that has to do with my five-year-old life"

Hyperbole

In "The First Day", Jones uses hyperbole to show how the daughter's mother was tenacious with enrolling her dughter in school. The words 'more' and 'ever', in which indicate something bigger (more) , and an instance of somthing that is unusual (ever).Then, he has the main character iterate through a list of the thing the mother takes out or enrollment, in which shows that the list is too long to count. Finally she uses the words 'five-year-old life' show how much of the papers was taken out in quantity.
