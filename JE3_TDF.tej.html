"Strewn about the floor are dozens and dozens of pieces of white paper, and people are walking over them without any thought of picking them up. And seeing this lack of concern all of a sudden I am afraid" 

Hyperbole, Alliteration

Jones uses hyperbole to show how careless people are at Seaton Elementary School. The words "dozens" used more than once is an example of repitition, in which can form a hyperbole when that word is referring to a plural entitie such as papers in groups of 12. the hyperbole is also followed by the word "afraid".  What that means is that the daughter is nervous about school due to the fact that no one cared about the papers being carelessly left on the floor.
