The Reason Mass Media Exists

                               Have you ever had a dream that was ever “deferred” ?The Langston Hughes poem may

have some truth for when Engineering tries to make Mass Media a thing past.There are many benefits that could

be true for people who take Mass Media as a STEM Track. Many of our 9th and 10th graders will or have had this

experience before.  The reason Mass Media exists and should exist is as it is created to help us become better

journalists, better bloggers, and better writers in the future.

                               The Mass Media STEM Track would make excellent journalists if salvaged. Mass Media is the

practice or art of getting media and information to the masses for good use and for notification in case it is

serious (breaking news). Mass Media can help those students who take this STEM track seriously get into other

fields of study such as meteorology or sports analysis. Mass Media also can help those students find internships

that they could never have: WRC for the summer, WJLA for the summer, WTTG or the summer, or WUSA for the

summer. That is a big help whe you want to become a journalist. 

                                The Mass Media STEM Track should and does exist to make students who take that STEM

Track into  better bloggers. I remember when I was in Summer Bridge in 2012. That time I had learned about

many Mass Media concepts, such as blogging and writing in the journalistic sense. The thing about Mass Media is

that it makes you a better blogger, Tweeter and  a better journalist.

                Mass Media also makes  you a better blogger. As I said before , I have learned about blogging in

Summer Bridge, along with the concepts of making successful blog posts for many people. A blog post can be

seen by many people if they have lots of followers and a popular hashtag. The also are blogging in other big

ways to the masses.

                       The Mass Media STEM Track can also make better writers. For example: Mass Media students can

interview, write editorials, news features and more.  These students were taught this to make them better

writers, and better at gathering information for biographies, esaays, reasearch papers and more. The thing about

Mass Media is that it can help start a really good stem journal provided the students writing it have the proper

journalistic skills to do it.

                       The Mass Media STEM Track should not be erradicated as it provides students the journalistic

skills that can make them better writers, bloggers, and journalists. So, Mr. Lesley, pleason don't defer the

dreams of many freshmen and incoming freshmen.  If you want to make our school better, why

 not make that STEM Track run the school newspaper, a TV Station, or a online newspaper? With IT helping Mass

Media, there could be a special WordPress site for that very purpose.
